LinkedIn Post 1

Title

How to Position Your Company: Ask Your ICPs

Source:

https://www.linkedin.com/posts/peeplaja_the-key-question-in-positioning-isnt-what-activity-7381282181601525760-baFk

Summary

Peep argues that positioning should start with understanding how customers naturally categorize your product. Rather than inventing new categories, companies should ask their ideal customers what type of solution they believe the product is and then build differentiation within that existing category.

Key Takeaway

Effective positioning begins with customer perception, not internal assumptions.

LinkedIn Post 2

Title

Observation About Positioning Your Company as "The Only"

Source:

https://www.linkedin.com/posts/peeplaja_observation-about-positioning-your-company-activity-7373408781235400704-pDDs

Summary

Peep explains that claims such as "we are the only solution" often trigger skepticism. Instead, marketers should highlight unique attributes and allow buyers to reach the conclusion themselves through research and evaluation.

Key Takeaway

Positioning works best when customers discover your uniqueness rather than being told about it directly.

LinkedIn Post 3

Title

How To Stand Out As A Challenger Brand In B2B

Source:

https://www.linkedin.com/posts/peeplaja_i-tested-this-at-saastock-and-the-results-activity-7329098349037445120-RlEI

Summary

Peep highlights research showing that B2B buyers typically remember only a few brands in a category and often purchase from companies already on their initial shortlist. He argues that challenger brands must invest in awareness and mental availability long before buyers enter the market.

Key Takeaway

Brand awareness influences whether a company is considered at all during the buying process.
