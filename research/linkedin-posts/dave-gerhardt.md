# Dave Gerhardt

## Why Selected

Dave Gerhardt is one of the most respected voices in B2B SaaS marketing. He is known for building marketing communities, content-led growth strategies, and brand-driven demand generation. He has held leadership positions at Drift and Privy and frequently shares practical advice on LinkedIn.

LinkedIn Profile:
https://www.linkedin.com/in/davegerhardt/



LinkedIn Post 1

Title: Brand Drives Demand

Source:
https://www.linkedin.com/posts/davegerhardt_brand-drives-demand-its-not-demand-gen-activity-7318641726728900611-579Q?utm


Summary:

Dave argues that brand marketing and demand generation are not opposing strategies. A strong brand makes every growth activity—sales, outbound outreach, and demand generation—more effective. He believes that as AI creates product similarity across markets, brand reputation will become one of the few remaining differentiators.

Key Takeaway:

Strong brands reduce friction across the entire customer acquisition process and become a long-term competitive advantage.


LinkedIn Post 2

Title: My Number One B2B Marketing Tip

Source:
https://www.linkedin.com/posts/davegerhardt_my-number-one-all-time-b2b-marketing-tip-activity-7420793747325554688-rdFf?utm


Summary:

Dave encourages marketers to stop thinking in terms of "B2B" versus "B2C" marketing. Instead, he suggests focusing on universal principles: capturing attention, standing out from competitors, creating memorable experiences, and understanding what genuinely interests people. Great marketing can be learned from any industry.

Key Takeaway:

The best marketers study human behavior rather than limiting themselves to industry-specific marketing tactics.

LinkedIn Post 3

Title: Measurement Kills Marketing

Source:
https://www.linkedin.com/posts/davegerhardt_measurement-kills-marketing-every-linkedin-activity-7216029499551793152-mPro?utm



Summary:

Dave explains that not every marketing activity can be measured directly. LinkedIn posts, podcasts, videos, newsletters, and communities all contribute to building trust over time. He compares these activities to making small deposits into a bucket that eventually generates customer demand and sales.

Key Takeaway:

Marketing effectiveness often comes from long-term brand building rather than immediate, perfectly attributable conversions.
