Summary

In this interview, Tas Bober discusses why many B2B landing pages fail despite following common marketing "best practices." She argues that marketers often focus on internal opinions, stakeholder preferences, and generic templates rather than actual buyer behavior. Tas emphasizes the importance of understanding user intent, using qualitative research, analyzing page interactions, and building landing pages around real customer needs rather than assumptions. She also explains how landing pages can serve as testing grounds for broader website and marketing strategy improvements.

Key Takeaways

Most B2B landing pages fail because they prioritize company assumptions over buyer behavior.
User intent should guide landing page design and messaging.
Qualitative insights often reveal more than simple conversion metrics.
Testing and iteration should be continuous rather than based on opinions.
Landing pages can help uncover insights that improve an entire website strategy.

Relevance To LinkedIn Organic Content Strategy

Although the discussion focuses on landing pages, the underlying lesson applies directly to LinkedIn content strategy: understand audience intent, focus on customer problems, test continuously, and use audience behavior rather than assumptions to guide content decisions. These same principles help creators produce more relevant LinkedIn content and build stronger audience engagement.
